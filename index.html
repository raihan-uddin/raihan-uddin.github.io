Engineering Approach

How I think about building software

Good engineering is not just about writing code that works — it's about building systems that last, teams that ship, and products that scale.

01
🏗️

Design for Scale First

Every system I build is designed to handle 10x the current load. Database schemas, API contracts, and service boundaries are chosen with growth in mind — not retrofitted later.

02
🎯

Solve the Business Problem

Technology is a tool. I invest time understanding the actual business domain before writing a line of code. The best technical solution serves the business outcome.

03
⚡

Obsess Over Performance

Query optimization, indexing strategies, caching layers, and efficient data structures. I treat slow systems as bugs. Every millisecond at scale has business cost.

04
🤖

Automate the Repetitive

Manual processes are failure points. I identify repetitive workflows and automate them — inventory alerts, reorder triggers, report generation, deployment pipelines.

05
🔍

Make the Invisible Visible

Dashboards, analytics, and reporting transforms raw data into business insight. I build systems where operators always know what's happening and why.

06
🛡️

Build for Reliability

Enterprise systems cannot go down. I design for fault tolerance, implement proper error handling, and build monitoring in from day one — not as an afterthought.

07
👥

Lead Through Clarity

As a team lead, I communicate technical decisions in plain language, break down ambiguous requirements, and create systems that other engineers enjoy working in.

08
📐

Own the Full Stack

From Linux server configuration and Nginx setup to database design, API architecture, and frontend integration — I understand every layer and can work at any level.

AI & Forecasting Engine

Predicting medicine demand
before it happens

One of my most impactful engineering challenges: building an AI-driven inventory forecasting system for pharmaceutical distribution. Traditional reorder systems react to stockouts. Mine predicts them — using historical sales data, seasonal patterns, and demand signals to recommend optimal stock levels weeks in advance.

✓
SARIMA time-series forecasting models trained on medicine sales history
✓
Seasonal demand signal detection (disease patterns, weather, festivals)
✓
Per-SKU and per-territory demand planning at scale
✓
Automated reorder recommendation engine integrated into ERP
✓
Dead stock and overstock prevention through predictive alerts

Forecasting Pipeline

📊
Sales History Ingestion
Collect 12–24 months of per-SKU, per-territory transactional sales data from ERP
🔍
Signal Detection
Identify seasonal patterns, demand spikes, and external signals (disease cycles, market trends)
🤖
SARIMA Model Inference
Seasonal ARIMA models compute 4–12 week forward demand forecast per SKU
⚖️
Safety Stock Calculation
Compute optimal reorder quantities accounting for lead time, variability, and service-level targets
📦
Purchase Order Recommendations
Automated PO suggestions surfaced in ERP dashboard — human-reviewed and one-click approved

Result

Significant reduction in stockouts and dead stock. Procurement teams now plan proactively instead of reacting to shortages.
